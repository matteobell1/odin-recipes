Spaghetti allo Scoglio

Description

Spaghetti allo Scoglio is a traditional Italian pasta dish that originated in the coastal regions of Italy. This delicious seafood pasta is made with fresh, plump mussels, succulent clams, tender shrimp, and sweet calamari, all saut√©ed in a garlicky tomato sauce with a hint of spicy chili flakes. The spaghetti is cooked al dente and tossed with the seafood and sauce, creating a perfect blend of flavors that transport you to the Mediterranean coast.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) of spaghetti
  • 1 pound (450g) of mixed seafood (mussels, clams, shrimp, and calamari)
  • 2 cups (480ml) of canned tomatoes
  • 4 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) of extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) of white wine
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1g) of red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A handful of fresh parsley, finely chopped

How to prepare

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent.
  4. Add the canned tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the seafood and white wine, and cook until the seafood is cooked through and the wine has evaporated.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Add the spaghetti to the skillet and toss to combine with the sauce.
  8. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot.
